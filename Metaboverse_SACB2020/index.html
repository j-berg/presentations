Gazing into the Metaboverse

Metabolic network pattern recognition tools contextualize multi-omic data and reveal disease-relevant signatures

Jordan A. Berg, Youjia Zhou, Yeyun Ouyang, T. Cameron Waller, Sara M. Nowinski, Tyler Van Ry, Ian George, James E. Cox, Bei Wang, Jared Rutter

Abstract

Due to the scale and complexity inherent in metabolism, systems approaches that are easily accessible to the general researcher are necessary. However, current conventions often drive scientists to limit their focus to independent, high-magnitude components from their datasets or do not easily facilitate the comprehensive analysis of the data in the context of the metabolic network. Thus, we developed Metaboverse, a user-friendly, interactive, and cross-platform analysis and visualization tool for data layered on metabolic networks. Metaboverse offers the ability to integrate multi-omic, multi-condition, time course, and metabolomics tracing datasets on over ninety model organisms. We developed a search engine that comprehensively searches and analyzes the network for complex regulatory patterns within the sequencing, proteomics, and metabolomics data, integrating enzyme information into the metabolic network to create a robust search space. Additionally, as measurement sparsity often presents a unique challenge in metabolism data analysis, we developed a method for summarizing reactions across missing values to allow for pattern searching across these spaces. Users can also explore the upstream effects and downstream consequences of a perturbation across the global network or analyze the extent of reaction perturbations on the metabolic network. The results of these search algorithms are returned to the user and help ensure they consider all available patterns within the data, not just those within the favorite or familiar pathways. These capabilities aid scientists in rapidly generating new hypotheses that may have been missed by available approaches. This framework will be foundational in allowing users to analyze their data in a more holistic manner that integrates all measured information of their biological system to rapidly identify interesting regulatory patterns within the global metabolic network. Metaboverse is available under a GPL-3.0 open-source license and a beta version can be found at https://github.com/Metaboverse/Metaboverse.
